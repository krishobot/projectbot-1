# Launch week 1 — astack packs ship-light

Soft launch to personal audience. Goal for the 7-day window: **first paid sale + signal on which pack resonates** (agency vs technical-founder vs bundle).

Voice: **founder's personal voice** (direct, terse, no AI vocabulary, no em dashes). Audience: solopreneurs building anything, broadly. OnlyKrida stays in reserve for the eventual growth-pack launch.

Channels:
- **Primary: personal X** (founder account)
- **Secondary: personal LinkedIn**
- **Reserved (don't use yet): OnlyKrida IG, Product Hunt, Hacker News**

The astack agent that owns each step is named in brackets `[09 Marketing]` etc. Run those in a Claude Code session scoped to that team.

## Day 7 — X reply mode (1 hour, max 10 replies)

Sales agent finds the threads worth replying to. You write the actual reply (in your voice). The trick: be useful first, mention the pack only if the thread is exactly the use case.

### Search queries to run

In X search, look for tweets posted in the last 24 hours matching:

- `"solo agency" OR "solopreneur"` AND `"client report" OR "scope creep"`
- `"side project" OR "side saas"` AND `"feature requests" OR "roadmap"`
- `"i'm a solo founder"` AND replies asking for tools
- `"freelance"` AND `"retainer" OR "consulting"`
- `"shipped"` AND `"production"` AND tweets that mention solo founder context

For each thread that's genuinely about the pain your packs solve:

1. Reply with one specific helpful thing (a playbook tip, an ADR insight, a math line). NO buy link.
2. If the thread author replies engaged, second reply gets a soft mention: "I wrote this up as part of {pack-name}. Free to read here: tanirudh.gumroad.com/l/X — but the playbook is the point, not the pack."

The point: the conversation establishes you as a peer in the niche. The pack mention is incidental, not the goal.

### The Sales agent prompt

Paste into a Claude Code session scoped to **Sales / BD (10)**:

```
Find 5-10 X threads from the last 24 hours about solopreneurs, solo agencies,
or solo founders dealing with: scope creep, client reports, ADRs, feature
prioritisation, or shipping discipline.

For each thread, output:
- Thread URL
- One-line summary of what they're working through
- Whether the relevant pack is agency-pack, technical-founder-pack, or
  neither (skip the thread)
- A 2-3 sentence helpful reply in my voice (no buy link, no AI vocabulary,
  no em dashes). The reply should be a real insight from the relevant
  playbook, not a generic "I feel you" response.

I'll review and post manually. Don't auto-send anything.
```

## Day 8 — single pain-point tweet (no buy link)

Builds the audience around a problem, not a product. A reply-bait tweet, not a sales tweet.

```
i almost forgot a sponsor reply yesterday because the context was scattered across 4 tools.

every solopreneur i know has this exact moment once a week.

stop trying to remember things. start writing them down in one place. doesn't matter where as long as it's the same place every time.
```

No link. No buy CTA. Just the pain, observed honestly. People will reply with their own version of the moment. That's the goal.

If anyone replies with "what tool do you use for this?" — second reply links to the OSS repo (github), not the paid pack. Top of funnel feeds bottom of funnel later.

astack is open source. MIT. github.com/krishobot/projectbot-1

## Day 10 — first buyer response OR validation question

Two scenarios.

### Scenario A: first sale landed (1+ buyers in days 5-9)

Customer Success agent runs:

```
[11 Customer Success] prompt:
A buyer just paid $50/$80 for {pack}. Pull from the Gumroad order email
into brain/people/{handle}.md and brain/deals/{handle}-{date}.md. Then
draft a personal welcome DM I can send via Discord:

- Acknowledge the purchase by name
- Ask one specific question about their context (what motion they're
  running, what they hope the pack changes for them)
- Invite them to introduce themselves in #wins channel
- Offer to do a 15-min call this week if they want a specific tuning

No template-feeling phrases. Sound like a real human who built it.
```

After they reply (DM or Discord), with their permission, draft a quote-tweet of the launch thread:

```
quote-tweet draft:

first buyer. {one-line about what they bought + why}.

🟢 the brain is now seeded. happy to keep posting what i learn from
buyer #2, #3, #N.
```

### Scenario B: no sales yet (zero buyers in days 5-9)

Reply-bait tweet asking what would close the deal:

```
honest question for solopreneurs.

i shipped two paid packs at $50 last week. 0 sales so far.

what's the one thing about the landing page or the offer that's making you scroll past?

(reply with brutal honesty; first 5 replies get a free copy)
```

This converts the silence into signal. Even bad replies tell you which objection to address. The "free copy" offer costs you nothing (it's a digital download) and turns 5 critics into 5 buyers-in-the-wider-funnel.

- Which tweet/post drove the most clicks (signal: which message hooked)

mattered more than the rest, lead with it.

Output of this retro shapes week 2. Repeat the rhythm for 90 days, then run the day-90 decision (per the CEO plan).
